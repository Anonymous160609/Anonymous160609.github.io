Defination of ABM

Agent-based Model simulates individual bodies' (agents) movements and interactions within in a given environment (wiki). It emphasizes randomness in each interaction and helps understand detailed, specific situations which would not be examinated and discussed by triditioanl methodologies e.g. regression.

Brief of the Code

It is an agent-based model including animated displays, real-time statistics in model , and parameter passings in interface. It simulates the movements of sheep and wolf, as well as and grassland for a duration. Sheep and wolf can move around grassland. Sheep can consume and store grass, can also average their grass sotre with nearly individuals. Wolf can prey sheep, turn their colour into red, and stop sheep moving. Initializing the model.py in spyder, it will show its UI. Users can input parameters in the left panel then confirm through button, else click "run" to start with initial parameters. When it runs, it is expected to show interactions between sheep wolf grassland, and changes grassland, grass, and sheep corps by users, both in form of animation and statistics.
